Google Ads E-Commerce Q3 2023 – Q1 2024

From 1.1x to 4.2x ROAS: Rebuilding a Broken Google Shopping Account

Inherited a structurally broken Shopping account burning $12k/mo. Rebuilt the feed, restructured campaigns by margin tier, and implemented Target ROAS bidding — delivering 4.2x ROAS in 90 days.

4.2x Final ROAS
38% CPA Reduction
90 Days to Result
$12k Monthly Spend
01 — The Situation

A Budget Fire with No Clear Cause

The client came to me after 18 months of running Google Shopping internally. They were spending $12,000/month and generating roughly $13,200 in attributed revenue — a 1.1x ROAS that barely covered platform fees, let alone operating costs.

Their primary complaint: "we're spending more every month and getting less back." A two-hour audit revealed the root cause — no negative keyword list, all products lumped into a single catch-all campaign, and a Smart Shopping setup with zero feed optimization.

The Core Problem:

1 campaign, 0 negative keywords, unoptimized product feed, Smart Shopping with no conversion history = budget bleed with no data foundation to optimize from.

02 — The Strategy

Rebuild Before Scale

The instinct is always to optimize what exists. But when the foundation is broken, optimization just makes you fail faster. I made the call to pause and rebuild from scratch over a 3-week period before spending another dollar.

01

Product Feed Audit & Rebuild

Rewrote all product titles with front-loaded keywords, added GTINs, fixed attribute errors flagged in Merchant Center, and segmented by margin category.

02

Campaign Restructure by Margin Tier

Split products into High Margin, Mid Margin, and Clearance campaigns — each with its own ROAS target, budget, and negative keyword list seeded from 3 months of search term data.

03

Conversion Tracking Audit

Discovered double-counting in GTM — the purchase event was firing twice on confirmation pages due to a legacy Universal Analytics tag still active. Fixed via GTM and revalidated in GA4 DebugView.

04

Bidding Strategy Transition

Started on Manual CPC for 3 weeks to build clean conversion data, then transitioned to Target ROAS bidding with conservative targets — letting Google's algorithm optimize within guardrails.

03 — The Results

90 Days. 4.2x ROAS.

By week 6 the account had crossed 2.8x ROAS — already well above the prior 18 months of performance. By week 12 the High Margin campaign hit 4.2x ROAS, and the client approved a 35% budget increase into that segment.

The CPA reduction wasn't just about bidding — it was about feeding the algorithm clean data, proper attribution, and structured product groups that let it find the right buyers at the right price.

4.2x Final ROAS ↑ from 1.1x
38% CPA Reduction ↓ $42 → $26
+35% Budget Increase Approved Client confidence restored
0 Double-Counted Conversions ↓ from ~30%
04 — The Takeaway

What This Proves

The Lesson:

Most underperforming Google Ads accounts aren't a bidding problem — they're a data quality and structure problem. Fix the foundation first. Then scale.
